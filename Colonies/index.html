Getting Started in Jerry's Colonies

Jerry's Colonies is a multiplayer-oriented addon for Minecraft: Bedrock Edition that adds player-managed colonies to the game. The goal is to turn Minecraft into a civilization simulator where players can manage/build cities, create lore, and simulate diplomacy.

The First Step:

You are well on your way to growing a powerful civilization, but first you need to set up the basics. Craft a flag using 6 wool and a stick. This is what will create your colony.

Now you can place your flag! To do this, build a flag pole out of any vanilla fence and right click (mobile users: hold) to place the flag on the fence. If it doesn't work, your flag pole isn't tall enough, you are too far away, or you are not looking at the fence.

[WARNING] Many people struggle to place the flag and get an error saying they can't; if this happens to you you're likely just not tapping the right area on your screen — stand directly in front of the flagpole and try again.

After placing the flag you should be given a crown named after your colony. This crown is unique and cannot be replaced — take good care of it.

Make a Mayor's Table

To progress in the addon you will need to craft and place a Mayor's Table. It is the centralized workstation for managing all aspects of your colony. The Mayor's Table exposes menus for colony info, citizens, blueprints, diplomacy, and various settings — you may find the number of options overwhelming at first, but each menu is explained in the relevant pages of this wiki.

Mayor's Table

The Mayor's Table is the central workstation for your colony. From it you can view colony info, change flag color, manage members, buy NPC citizens, purchase blueprints, control diplomacy, rename or delete the colony, transfer ownership, and teleport between flags.

Citizens

There are two citizen types: Players and NPCs. Players are invited via the Mayor's Table. NPC citizens can be purchased (cost shown in the table, commonly 1 diamond each). Citizens perform jobs, gather resources, fight mobs, use equipment, and grant colony-wide bonuses.

Feeding Citizens

Citizens must be fed or they can die. Craft a food crate (chest + bread + planks) and fill it with food — citizens will take one food item per day from the crate.

Knights

Knights are a special citizen job. They are stronger than regular citizens and can be ordered to follow and assist you in combat.

War & Diplomacy

Use diplomacy to record relationships with other colonies (primarily for roleplay). To declare war you must meet the requirements (for example, have enough NPC citizens). Wars last 30 minutes; victory conditions include eliminating opposing NPCs or player citizens. Winners may claim flags and territory.

Blueprints & Construction

Buy blueprints at the Mayor's Table to instantly place complex structures (houses, farms, castles, roads). Placing a blueprint shows a preview outline; after a short preview you can confirm and build, with an option to undo. Boats and other special builds are also available.

Marauders & Random Events

Random events can affect colonies. Marauders arrive in hot-air balloons and may drop bombs (wave 1) then land and attack (wave 2). These raids are rare but dangerous.

Plagues

Plagues can infect a colony, applying negative effects (for example, a temporary weakness) to citizens for a limited time.

Land Protection & Moderation Tool

Each colony protects a radius (about 100 blocks) around its flag. Non-members cannot break blocks inside this area. The moderation tool (admin tag) exposes server admin options: delete/teleport to colonies, toggle modules, adjust prices, and set NPC caps or land-protection settings.

That's the essentials — use the Mayor's Table and flags to manage and grow your civilization, and join the Discord for suggestions or support.
